Addressing the earlier aces-signal critique

The previous aces-signal page noted: "The signal informs partner more than declarer — and partner mostly reacts in seat-3 (push to 5) or as dealer (take). The current strategy doesn't use the aces signal in those places, so the receiver-side gain here is partially wasted."

This page fixes that: the new bid3ReceiverSeat3 and bid3ReceiverDealer flags wire partner_bid == 3 into the seat-3 push rules and dealer-take rules, in addition to the trump selection rules. Tested at three ace-count thresholds (2, 3, 4) to isolate the "rare but highly specific" sweet spot.

Three orthogonal ideas are also on this page:

  • Sig-17 receiver boost — at sig=17, partner's bid 1 or bid 2 represents "3+ winners in the signaled direction". The receiver rules were tuned for Family's weaker king_ace_count() ≥ 3 semantics; this variant adds stronger push / take rules that exploit the higher-information signal.
  • Bid 4 on hand_power + length — if I have a sig-strong hand AND a 4 or 5-card suit, skip the bid 2/bid 1 signal and commit directly to bid 4. Tests whether some of those "signal 2 → partner pushes to 4" flows would be better served by a pre-committed bid 4.
  • Leading strongest non-trump — flip the final lead rule from hand.nontrump.weakest to hand.nontrump.strongest, per the "partner-of-declarer, on control, plays strongest-to-weakest excluding trump" heuristic some players use.
