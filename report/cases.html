What happens in concrete hands

Each case study below fixes Player 0's hand to a specific archetype (constructed via the 52-character deck URL), fills the rest of the deck with a fixed seeded shuffle, and simulates the hand under Family plus four Powered variants. The same physical deck is used across all five strategies, so the only varying factor is the bidding policy. This makes the causal chain — "threshold → signal → bid → declarer → result" — directly visible.

Important caveat: a single hand doesn't prove a strategy is better — the proof is in the aggregate (see Sweep Data). Individual hands can be bad luck (opponents happen to hold the right cards to break a strong hand) or good luck. These case studies exist to show the mechanism — where the signal changes who declares, what trump direction gets called, and so on — not to cherry-pick outcomes. Look especially at the Q/J-stack and AAKQ cases, where the strategies diverge clearly between "P0 becomes declarer and fails" (low sig) and "partner declares and makes" (high sig).

Open any hand in the app via the localhost link to step through the game manually.
