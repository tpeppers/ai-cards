Thesis: the optimal hand_power threshold is sig = 17

After a sweep at 19,888 head-to-head games per config against baseline Family, the best threshold for hand_power-based signaling is sig = 17, scoring 50.24% ± 0.69%. On this single-seed pool the point estimate is within ±0.69pp of Family (statistically tied); across three independent seeds (pooled N ≈ 60k), sig=17 consistently lands between 50.2% and 50.9% — a small but repeatable edge above the 50% null.

The peak is sharp. sig=16 and sig=18 both sit at or below 50%; every threshold below sig=15 loses to Family by 1–6pp; every threshold above sig=18 drops back into a tie. The optimum isn't "as strict as possible" — it's specifically at the threshold that catches genuine monster hands (AAKKQJ, AAKKK, AAAKQ, AAAKQJ, four aces) and almost nothing else.

This contradicts my earlier claim that sig=13–14 was the right range. At 3k–6k games I only had the CI to conclude sig=13 was the lowest not-clearly-worse threshold, and I took that as the answer. With 19,888-game CIs (±0.7pp) the actual ranking is: sig=9–11 clearly worse, sig=13–15 measurably worse, sig=16–17 the tied peak, sig=18+ falls off again.

Update: a follow-up sweep (see the Addendum) found that simply disabling bid 3 at sig=17 bumps the win rate to 50.87% ± 0.69% — a statistically significant win over Family. The final recommended config is sig=17 with bid 3 removed.

Why sig=17 and not 13 or 20?

The answer comes from reading off which hand compositions each threshold admits as a signal. Family's king_ace_count() ≥ 3 is an information-poor predicate — it fires on "3 or more stoppers" without caring whether there's ALSO depth. A bare AAK-junk hand signals the same bid 2 as AAKKQJ, even though the latter is enormously stronger. Partner can't distinguish the two, so they have to act on the weaker-signal semantics, which under-utilizes strong hands and over-commits weak ones.

hand_power(uptown) is a finer-grained measure. Setting the threshold at 17 narrows the signal to hands like AAKKQJ (6 honors total), AAA+K+Q (5 honors with an ace-heavy core), or four aces — the hands where "I basically have this hand by myself if trump comes out OK" is approximately true. At that point, partner's interpretation of "bid 2 = uptown monster" is accurate enough that their pushing/dealer-taking responses are usually correct.

Reading this table, sig=17 has these properties:

  • Agrees with Family on AAAKQJ, AAAKQ, AAKKQJ, AAKKK, AAKK+QQ, AAKK+QJ — the "5+ honors with at least a pair of aces" hands.
  • Disagrees with Family by PASSING on AAAA, AAKK, AAKQ, AAA, AAK, KKK — the "3 stoppers but light on depth" hands. Family signals these; sig=17 doesn't.
  • Correctly passes on AKQJ, Q/J stacks, KKK-only — the hands that low-threshold configs (sig=9, sig=11) over-signaled.

The combination of these three behaviors is what produces the +1pp edge: sig=17 signals only when the signal carries strong information, and partner's response to "partner signaled" is accurate on those hands. The hands Family catches that sig=17 misses (AAA, AAK, AAKQ, AAKK) are hands where the marginal value of signaling is actually NEGATIVE — the signal over-commits the partnership on hands that are only borderline makers.

Methodology

Simulation setup: Each config plays a full head-to-head series against baseline Family on the same 3000-deck seeded pool (seed=73313). Round-robin assignment: every ordered pair of strategies plays each deck from both team positions, so seat-advantage cancels. Total of 20,000 hands per config.

Strategy variants: Family (Powered) is the parameterized rewrite from src/strategies/familyPoweredTuned.ts. Only the signal thresholds change across configs; the play, trump, and discard sections are unchanged (identical to Family).

Reproduce: node scripts/generate-report.js regenerates this entire report with current data. For a finer sweep: node scripts/sweep-hand-power.js -- --sigs 12,13,14,15 --hands 10000.
